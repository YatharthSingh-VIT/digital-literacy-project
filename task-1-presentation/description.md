# Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

## Tool Used
Canva (canva.com) — free browser-based graphic design platform

---

## About the Infographic

This is a single page vertical infographic I made for the
Student Digital Ambassador project. The idea was simple —
make something a batchmate could look at for 30 seconds
and actually take something away from it. No walls of text,
no complicated diagrams. Just clear sections with icons
and short bullet points.

I used Canva's Magic Design feature. Typed in a prompt
describing what I needed, it generated a base template,
and I edited it from there. The kraft paper background
wasn't something I planned — it just came out of the
prompt and I liked it so I kept it.

---

## Topics Covered

**1. Understanding Digital Facets**
What digital literacy actually means in practice —
finding and evaluating information, creating original
content using digital tools, and understanding things
like copyright and distribution ethics.

**2. Key Learning and Collaboration Tools**
The platforms most students already use but maybe don't
use properly — Google Classroom for assignments,
Google Docs for real-time collaboration, YouTube for
learning, Zoom for virtual classes.

**3. Navigating Internet Safety**
The basics that honestly everyone should know —
strong passwords, recognising phishing emails, keeping
privacy settings updated, and checking for HTTPS before
entering any personal information on a website.

**4. Building a Positive Digital Presence**
This one felt personal to write. Mindful posting,
professional networking, monitoring what's out there
about you online, and just being a decent person in
how you interact on the internet.

**5. Mastering Email Etiquette**
Clear subject lines, proper greetings, getting to the
point in the body, ending professionally, and actually
proofreading before hitting send. Sounds obvious.
A lot of people skip it.

---

## Design Choices

- **Background:** Vintage kraft paper texture — stands
  out compared to the usual plain white infographics
- **Headings:** Bold yellow stamp-style font — easy to
  scan quickly when reading top to bottom
- **Icons:** Blue flat illustrated icons for each section
- **Layout:** Alternating icons left, text right pattern
  so it doesn't feel like a boring list
- **Bottom:** My name and reg number included at the
  bottom right as required

---

## What I Found Difficult

Getting the prompt right honestly took a few attempts.
The first couple of generations had the icons in the
wrong places and the sections felt cramped. Once I got
more specific about the alternating layout and the
kraft paper style it came out much better. Also had
to fix a spelling mistake in one of the headings
before downloading the final version.
