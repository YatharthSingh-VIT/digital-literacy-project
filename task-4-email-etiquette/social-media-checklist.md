# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### CSE0001 Digital Literacy | Yatharth Singh | 25BAI10657

---

## DO's ✅

1. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself if you'd be okay
   with your professor or future employer seeing it.
   If the answer is no, just don't.

2. **Keep your LinkedIn updated** — Even as a first year student,
   a clean LinkedIn with your education and interests goes
   a long way.

3. **Use social media to learn** — Follow people in your field,
   read what professionals post, join communities related
   to AI, tech or whatever genuinely interests you.

4. **Give credit when you share** — If you're reposting someone's
   work, idea or photo — mention them. Its basic respect.

5. **Keep your privacy settings in check** — Review who can see
   your posts, your location, your stories. Do it once
   every few months at least.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. **Don't post in anger** — Venting online feels good for
   about 5 minutes. The post stays forever. Sleep on it.

2. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Your phone
   number, home address, daily schedule — none of this
   should be on a public profile.

3. **Don't engage with or spread fake news** — If something
   sounds too dramatic to be true, it probably is.
   Check before sharing.

4. **Don't use the same password across platforms** — If one
   account gets hacked, all of them go down. Use different
   passwords or a password manager.

5. **Don't ignore someone who is being bullied online** —
   You don't have to fight anyone. But reporting it or
   checking in on the person actually matters.
