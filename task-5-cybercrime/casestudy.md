# Ransomware — Cybercrime Case Study

---

## What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software that locks you out
of your own device or files and then demands money — usually
in cryptocurrency — to give you access back. Think of it like
someone breaking into your house, changing all the locks, and
then sliding a note under the door asking for payment.

---

## How It Happened

Rohan was a third year engineering student who used his laptop
for everything. Notes, assignments, project files, internship
documents — all of it was on there. No backup. Never seemed
necessary.

One evening he got an email that looked like it was from his
university's admin portal. Something about updating his exam
registration. He clicked the link, downloaded what he thought
was a form, and opened it.

Nothing seemed to happen. He forgot about it.

The next morning he turned on his laptop. Every single file
had been renamed with strange extensions he didn't recognise.
A text file had appeared on his desktop. It said his files
were encrypted and he had 48 hours to pay Rs. 30,000 in
Bitcoin or they would be permanently deleted.

Rohan panicked. He had his final year project files on there.
Three months of work. He didn't know what Bitcoin was.
He didn't know who to call.

---

## Who Gets Targeted?

Ransomware doesn't discriminate. Students, small business
owners, hospitals, government departments — anyone who stores
important files without proper backups is a potential target.
Students are especially vulnerable because they rarely back up
their work and tend to click on things without thinking too
hard about it.

---

## Consequences

Rohan lost most of his files. He reported it to cybercrime
authorities but recovery wasn't possible. He had to redo
significant portions of his final year project. Beyond the
academic damage the psychological impact was real — the
anxiety, the embarrassment, the feeling of being completely
helpless.

His only mistake was clicking one link without checking where
it actually came from.
