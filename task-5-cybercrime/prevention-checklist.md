# Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist

---

## General Online Safety

1. **Think before you click**
   If an email, message or link feels even slightly off —
   stop. Verify first. One wrong click is genuinely all
   it takes. Rohan's story is proof of that.

2. **Keep your software and apps updated**
   Those update notifications you keep dismissing? They
   often contain security fixes. Ignoring them leaves
   gaps that attackers actively look for.

3. **Use strong, different passwords for every account**
   "123456" and your date of birth are not passwords.
   Use a mix of letters, numbers and symbols. And please
   don't use the same one everywhere.

4. **Enable Two Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible**
   Even if someone gets your password, 2FA means they
   still can't get in without your phone. Turn it on
   for email, Instagram, everything important.

5. **Back up your files regularly**
   An external hard drive or Google Drive. Anywhere.
   Just don't keep everything only on your laptop with
   no copy anywhere else. Rohan learnt this the hard way.

6. **Be careful what you download**
   Free movies, cracked software, random email
   attachments — these are the most common ways
   ransomware and malware enter your device.

7. **Check URLs before entering any personal information**
   A fake website can look identical to the real one.
   Always check that the URL starts with https and
   matches the official domain exactly.

---

## Financial and UPI Safety

8. **Never share your UPI PIN with anyone — ever**
   No bank, no app, no customer care representative
   will ever ask for your PIN. If someone does,
   hang up immediately. It is always a scam.

9. **Double check the UPI ID before sending money**
   One wrong character and your money goes to a
   completely different person. There is no undo
   button on UPI transactions. Check. Then check again.

10. **Be suspicious of any "you've won a prize" messages**
    If someone asks you to scan a QR code or enter
    your UPI PIN to receive money — that is a scam.
    Receiving money never requires your PIN.

---

## If Something Goes Wrong

- **Report cybercrime immediately at:** cybercrime.gov.in
- **Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930 (available 24x7)
- Don't panic, don't pay any ransom, and don't delete
  anything before reporting — evidence matters.
